# Product

## Register

product

## Users
University professors evaluating and grading students on their implementation of multimedia compression algorithms.

## Product Purpose
To clearly, cleanly, and interactively demonstrate lossless compression algorithms (RLE, Huffman, LZW, etc.) with measurable results. Success means the professor immediately understands the inputs, outputs, and compression ratios without friction.

## Brand Personality
Clean, modern, minimalist, and highly functional. It should feel like Notion — highly refined, typography-driven, effortless to use, with subtle and purposeful animations.

## Anti-references
- Streamlit-style generic data dashboards.
- Bulky, unstyled "academic project" interfaces.
- Cluttered, over-engineered layouts.

## Design Principles
- **Clarity over cleverness:** The algorithms are the star; the UI should get out of the way.
- **Notion-like precision:** Use subtle lines, plenty of whitespace, and clear hierarchy.
- **Immediate validation:** Before/After states and compression ratios must be instantly visible and comparable.
- **Purposeful motion:** Use smooth, meaningful animations to show state changes (e.g., when compression completes).

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Standard web accessibility (no special requirements).
